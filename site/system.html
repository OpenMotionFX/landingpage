Hardware Architecture

Eight Modules.
One Brain.

Every node in the OpenMotionFX ecosystem runs on the exact same microcontroller: the ESP32-S3. There is no "master controller" box to buy. The intelligence is distributed, meaning a single pan/tilt head is a complete system on its own. Everything else is additive. 

omfx-net --scan-nodes

01. Pan/Tilt Head

The flagship of the project. Designed specifically to handle the payload of an iPhone 17 Pro Max or a stripped-down mirrorless camera. By utilizing zero-backlash printed cycloidal gears and closed-loop NEMA17 motors, it hits repeatability tolerances previously locked behind $3,000 price tags.

Engineering Specs
Parameter Value
Max Payload 1,200 g (2.6 lbs)
Repeatability 0.02° variance
Drive System NEMA17 / Cycloidal
Est. Build Cost $75–95

02. Slider

A 500 mm linear rail driven by a GT2 belt system. Built entirely from standard 3D-printer supply chain parts, keeping costs minimal while ensuring worldwide part availability. Synchronizes seamlessly with the Pan/Tilt head to create multi-axis tracking shots.

Engineering Specs
Parameter Value
Track Length 500 mm (Expandable)
Repeatability 0.05 mm variance
Max Speed 200 mm/sec
Est. Build Cost $90–110

03. Focus / Zoom Motor

Turns standard cinema lens gears into a programmed axis. Perfect for racking focus on a dolly-in, creating repeatable Vertigo effects, or locking focus planes on macro product spins. Records directly into the exported `.omfx` move file for VFX ingestion.

Engineering Specs
Parameter Value
Gear Pitch 0.8 MOD (Cinema Standard)
Torque 1.2 Nm
Calibration Auto hard-stop detection
Est. Build Cost $35–50

04. Lighting & Sync

Motion control isn't just about the camera. The Key Light, FX Light, and Sync Box modules ensure your lighting shifts frame-accurately with the camera move. Replicate police strobes, flicker-free fires, or trigger camera shutters via precise timecode.
